The rules of dibs


  • As a rule of thumb, “dibs” will always go to the person who “calls them” first.
  • In order for it to be legally binding, you must say the full statement of what you call dibs on
  • In order to be fair, a dibs will only last a week on a given object
  • You must be in the same room or same conversation to call dibs.
  • The statement, oral or written, must be complete without any mistakes or stuters to confirm the dibs
  • Any mispelling or mispronounciation renders the dib useless

Unknown to most people, there are also the rules of debs

These rules are for those that do mispronounce/mispell 'dibs' and has a completly different set of rules and are as follows:

  • After a person calls 'debs' they waive the right to something if someone calls 'dibs' on it
  • If no one counters the point of debs, then it will act as a normal dibs, but only last for a day
  • If a person does call debs, then they are entitled to a joke
